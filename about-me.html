Hello! I am Lorrie R. Chesnut, a certified editor, writer, and document designer who loves the written English language. Yes, I was that kid who ruined her eyes reading by flashlight under the covers. At a young age, creative teachers inspired my writing as a way to express and explore my world. My friends thought me weird because I would comment on how sentences sound and kept a list of favorite first lines.

Post high school, my aspirations took me into nursing, but life has a way of changing courses, and I spent time raising a family before returning to the university to earn my degree in English and Creative Writing. I joined a speculative fiction magazine and worked as an editor, managing editor, and finally editor-in-chief. I am proud to say that Covid tried to shut us down, but we had it to press before it could. The challenge came in getting our orders filled while social distancing. This experience refined my editing and document design abilities. Surprisingly, managing a family and a journal were shockingly similar, yet satisfyingly enjoyable.

After graduation, I worked for three years with a small press publisher, handling developmental and substantive editing, copyediting, proofreading, and collaborative ghostwriting. My projects included suspense and fantasy novels, book synopses, and marketing blurbs.

In 2022, I joined Terracon Consultants Inc as a technical editor. To be honest, this meant editing a lot of reports about asbestos and sandy silty soils (say that five times fast). The relationship with environmental scientists and geotechnical engineers helped me understand the technical aspects of their writing. Where literary writing has a lot of flexibility, technical writing requires an eye to boilerplate and specific language. The team aspect of working with project managers and subject matter experts improved my editing process. Most projects were under tight deadlines and required organization and planning.

The company underwent a major rebrand during this time. This included creating templates, developing resume and proposal content, managing social media marketing, and writing/editing successful EPA Brownfields grants. The opportunity to combine creativity with meticulous organization taught me a great deal as both a writer and an editor. I prided myself on ensuring deliverables were concise, technically accurate, and aesthetically pleasing.

There were opportunities for marketing experience, but the job became more marketing and less writing/editing. Driven by a core belief that every piece of writing deserves to be thoughtfully crafted and authentically represent its creator; I decided to embark on this new path. If you value well-expressed ideas as much as I do, let's get together and bring your visions to life through the written word.
